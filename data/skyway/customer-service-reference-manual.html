SkyWay Airlines

Customer Service Reference Manual — Version 3.2 — Effective 1 March 2026

This document is the authoritative reference for SkyWay Airlines customer service representatives and automated customer-facing systems. All policies described herein apply to flights operated by SkyWay Airlines (flight numbers SW0001–SW9999). Codeshare flights operated by partner airlines are governed by the operating carrier's policies unless explicitly stated otherwise.

1. Booking and Reservations

1.1. Fare Classes

SkyWay Airlines offers four cabin classes, each with distinct service levels and flexibility:

Economy Class

The standard cabin class available on all SkyWay routes. Economy fares are divided into three sub-categories:

  • Economy Light — the most restrictive fare. No checked baggage included. Seat selection available for a fee. Non-refundable. Changes permitted up to 24 hours before departure for a fee of EUR 75 per segment in addition to fare difference.
  • Economy Standard — includes one checked bag (23 kg). Seat selection included in rows 15 and above. Non-refundable, but a travel voucher may be issued for cancellations made more than 48 hours before departure. Changes permitted for a fee of EUR 50 per segment, in addition to fare difference.
  • Economy Flex — includes one checked bag (23 kg). Free seat selection in all economy rows. Fully refundable up to 24 hours before departure. Free changes up to 3 hours before departure, subject to fare difference.

Premium Economy Class

Available on medium-haul and long-haul routes (flights over 4 hours). Premium Economy offers a wider seat (48 cm vs 44 cm in Economy), increased recline, and an enhanced meal service. Includes one checked bag (23 kg) and priority boarding. Seat selection is free. Changes permitted free of charge up to 24 hours before departure, subject to fare difference. Refundable up to 48 hours before departure.

Business Class

Available on all routes over 2 hours. Business Class provides a lie-flat seat on long-haul routes (over 6 hours) and a recliner seat on medium-haul routes. Service includes a multi-course meal, lounge access at departure, priority check-in, and priority boarding. Includes two checked bags (32 kg each). Free seat selection. Fully refundable. Free unlimited changes subject to fare difference.

First Class

Available on select long-haul routes between hub airports (Frankfurt, Dubai, Tokyo Narita, New York JFK, and Singapore Changi). First Class provides a private suite with a closing door, a full-length lie-flat bed, and à la carte dining. Service includes chauffeur transfer within 80 km of the departure or arrival airport, exclusive lounge access, private check-in, and priority boarding. Includes three checked bags (32 kg each). Fully refundable. Free unlimited changes including date, route, and cabin class (upgrade only; downgrade refunds the difference).

1.2. Booking Channels

Passengers may book SkyWay Airlines flights through the following channels:

  • SkyWay website (www.skyway-airlines.example) — full fare selection, seat maps, and ancillary services. Bookings receive an immediate e-ticket and confirmation email.
  • SkyWay mobile app (iOS and Android) — same functionality as the website with the addition of mobile boarding passes and push notifications for flight status changes.
  • SkyWay Customer Service Centre — available by phone 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. A service fee of EUR 25 per booking applies for phone reservations except for passengers requiring special assistance (see Section 7).
  • Travel agencies and online travel agents (OTAs) — fares and conditions may vary. Ancillary services purchased through third parties must be managed through that third party. SkyWay cannot modify ancillary bookings made through external channels.
  • Airport ticket offices — available at all SkyWay hub airports during operating hours. Walk-up fares may be higher than advance-purchase fares.

1.3. Booking Confirmation and Ticketing

Upon completing a booking, passengers receive a Booking Reference (also called a PNR — Passenger Name Record), which is a six-character alphanumeric code. This reference is required for all subsequent interactions including check-in, changes, and cancellations.

E-tickets are issued automatically for bookings made through SkyWay direct channels (website, app, and Customer Service Centre). For bookings through travel agents, the agent is responsible for issuing the e-ticket within the ticketing time limit, which is typically 24 hours after booking but may vary depending on the fare rules.

Failure to ticket within the time limit results in automatic cancellation of the booking without notification.

1.4. Name Changes and Corrections

SkyWay Airlines distinguishes between name corrections and name changes:

  • Name correction — fixing a minor spelling error (up to 3 characters) in the passenger's name. Free of charge when requested at least 48 hours before departure. Must be requested through the Customer Service Centre or at an airport ticket office.
  • Name change — transferring the booking to a different person. Not permitted on any fare class. The existing booking must be cancelled (subject to cancellation rules of the fare class) and a new booking made.

The name on the booking must match the name on the travel document (passport or ID card) that will be presented at the airport. Minor discrepancies (e.g., middle name omitted, use of a nickname that appears on the passport) are accepted at the discretion of check-in staff but SkyWay cannot guarantee boarding if the names do not match.

1.5. Infant and Child Policies

Infants (under 2 years)

Infants who have not reached their second birthday on the date of the outbound flight may travel on an adult's lap at a reduced fare (10% of the adult fare on domestic and European routes; 10% of the adult fare plus applicable taxes on long-haul routes). No baggage allowance is included for lap infants, but one collapsible stroller or car seat may be checked free of charge. One infant per accompanying adult.

If a bassinet is required for long-haul flights, it must be requested at least 72 hours before departure through the Customer Service Centre. Bassinets are available in bulkhead rows only and are subject to availability. Weight limit for bassinet use is 11 kg.

Children (2–11 years)

Children aged 2 to 11 must occupy their own seat and pay the applicable child fare, which is typically the same as the adult fare for the selected cabin class. Children under 5 must be accompanied by a passenger aged 16 or older. Children aged 5 to 11 may travel as unaccompanied minors (see Section 7.2).

1.6. Group Bookings

Groups of 10 or more passengers travelling on the same itinerary may request a group fare through the SkyWay Groups Desk (groups@skyway-airlines.example). Group bookings require a deposit of 10% of the total fare at the time of booking, with full payment due at least 30 days before departure. Passenger names may be added or changed up to 14 days before departure at no extra charge, after which standard name correction rules apply. Group bookings are non-refundable, but date changes may be permitted subject to availability and a per-passenger administration fee of EUR 30.

2. Check-in

2.1. Online Check-in

Online check-in is available through the SkyWay website and mobile app from 48 hours to 1 hour before the scheduled departure time. During online check-in, passengers can:

  • Confirm or change their seat assignment (subject to availability and fare rules)
  • Add checked baggage (if not included in the fare)
  • Request special meals (if not already requested — must be done at least 24 hours before departure; requests made during online check-in cannot be guaranteed)
  • Download or email a boarding pass

Online check-in is not available for:

  • Passengers requiring special assistance who have not pre-arranged their requirements
  • Passengers travelling with live animals in the cabin
  • Passengers on codeshare flights operated by partner airlines (check-in is handled by the operating carrier)
  • Group bookings with more than 9 passengers

2.2. Mobile Check-in

Mobile check-in through the SkyWay app follows the same window as online check-in (48 hours to 1 hour before departure). The app generates a mobile boarding pass with a QR code that can be scanned at security checkpoints and boarding gates. Mobile boarding passes are accepted at all SkyWay airports.

Passengers using mobile boarding passes must still visit the baggage drop counter if they have checked baggage, unless they have used a self-service bag drop kiosk (available at Frankfurt, Munich, London Heathrow, Dubai, and Singapore Changi).

2.3. Airport Check-in

Airport check-in counters open 3 hours before departure for long-haul flights and 2 hours before departure for short-haul and medium-haul flights. Check-in counters close:

  • 60 minutes before departure for long-haul flights
  • 45 minutes before departure for medium-haul flights
  • 40 minutes before departure for short-haul flights

Passengers who arrive after the check-in deadline will be denied boarding and their seat may be released to standby passengers. No refund or rebooking is provided for missed check-in deadlines, regardless of the fare class.

Business Class and First Class passengers have access to dedicated check-in counters with shorter queues. SkyWay Voyager Gold and Platinum members may also use the priority check-in counters regardless of cabin class.

2.4. Seat Selection

Seat selection policies vary by fare class:

  • Economy Light: seat selection available for a fee (EUR 8–25 depending on the route and seat location). If no seat is selected, one will be assigned at check-in.
  • Economy Standard: free seat selection in rows 15 and above. Preferred seats (extra legroom, exit rows) available for a fee of EUR 15–45.
  • Economy Flex: free seat selection in all economy rows including preferred seats.
  • Premium Economy: free seat selection in all Premium Economy rows.
  • Business Class: free seat selection in all Business Class rows.
  • First Class: free seat selection in all First Class suites.

Seat assignments are subject to change for operational or safety reasons. SkyWay Airlines reserves the right to reassign seats at any time. If a passenger is involuntarily downgraded to a lower cabin class, compensation is provided in accordance with EU Regulation 261/2004: 30% of the ticket price for flights up to 1500 km, 50% for flights between 1500 and 3500 km, and 75% for flights over 3500 km.

2.5. Travel Documents

Passengers are responsible for ensuring they hold valid travel documents for their destination and any transit points. Required documents may include:

  • A valid passport (with at least 6 months validity beyond the date of return for many destinations)
  • Visa or electronic travel authorization as required by the destination country
  • Health documentation (vaccination certificates, COVID-19 test results if still required by the destination)
  • Transit visa if connecting through a country that requires one

SkyWay Airlines may deny boarding if a passenger does not present valid travel documents. No refund is provided in such cases, regardless of the fare class.

3. Baggage

3.1. Carry-on Baggage

All passengers are permitted the following carry-on baggage:

  • One cabin bag with maximum dimensions of 55 × 40 × 23 cm and maximum weight of 8 kg. This bag must fit in the overhead compartment.
  • One personal item (handbag, laptop bag, or small backpack) with maximum dimensions of 40 × 30 × 15 cm. This item must fit under the seat in front of the passenger.

Business Class and First Class passengers are permitted two cabin bags (each within the dimensions above) plus one personal item.

Cabin baggage is checked at the gate. Bags exceeding the size or weight limit will be moved to the cargo hold and a checked baggage fee may be applied if no checked baggage allowance remains.

3.2. Checked Baggage Allowance

Checked baggage allowances vary by fare class and route:

Economy Light:

  • Short-haul and medium-haul (under 6 hours): no checked baggage included.
  • Long-haul (6 hours or more): one checked bag (23 kg) included.
  • Additional bags may be purchased: EUR 25 for the first bag (if not included), EUR 45 for the second bag, EUR 80 for each additional bag.

Economy Standard:

  • All routes: one checked bag (23 kg) included.
  • Additional bags: EUR 45 for the second bag, EUR 80 for each additional bag.

Economy Flex:

  • All routes: one checked bag (23 kg) included.
  • Additional bags: EUR 45 for the second bag, EUR 80 for each additional bag.

Premium Economy:

  • All routes: one checked bag (23 kg) included.
  • Additional bags: EUR 40 for the second bag, EUR 70 for each additional bag.

Business Class:

  • All routes: two checked bags (32 kg each) included.
  • Additional bags: EUR 60 for each additional bag.

First Class:

  • All routes: three checked bags (32 kg each) included.
  • Additional bags: EUR 60 for each additional bag.

Maximum dimensions for all checked bags: 158 cm total (length + width + height). No single checked bag may exceed 32 kg regardless of fare class. Bags exceeding 23 kg but not exceeding 32 kg incur a heavy bag surcharge of EUR 50 per bag (except for Business and First Class, where the 32 kg allowance is standard).

Pre-purchasing additional checked bags online is cheaper than purchasing at the airport. Online prices are approximately 30% lower than airport prices.

3.3. Excess and Oversize Baggage

Bags exceeding the maximum dimensions of 158 cm total (length + width + height) are classified as oversize baggage and incur a surcharge of EUR 100 per item per direction of travel, in addition to any applicable checked baggage fees. Oversize bags must be checked in at the oversized baggage counter (where available) or at the regular check-in counter.

Bags exceeding 32 kg cannot be checked as passenger baggage and must be shipped as cargo through SkyWay Cargo (cargo@skyway-airlines.example).

3.4. Special Items

The following items require advance notice and may incur additional fees:

Sports equipment:

  • Bicycles: EUR 60 per direction. Must be partially disassembled (handlebars turned, pedals removed) and packed in a bicycle box or padded bag. Maximum weight 32 kg.
  • Skis and snowboards: EUR 40 per direction. Must be in a ski bag. Counts as one piece of checked baggage if within the weight allowance.
  • Golf equipment: EUR 40 per direction. Must be in a golf travel bag. Counts as one piece of checked baggage if within the weight allowance.
  • Surfboards: EUR 80 per direction. Maximum length 300 cm.
  • Diving equipment: accepted as regular checked baggage if within dimensions and weight limits. Tanks must be empty and valves open.

Musical instruments:

  • Small instruments (violin, guitar, etc.) may be carried in the cabin if they fit in the overhead compartment or if an extra seat is purchased. Maximum dimensions for overhead storage: 130 × 45 × 25 cm.
  • Large instruments (cello, etc.) require the purchase of an additional seat. The instrument must be secured with the seat belt and must not exceed 75 kg.
  • Very large instruments (double bass, harp, etc.) must be shipped as cargo.

Mobility aids:

  • Wheelchairs and mobility scooters are transported free of charge in addition to the regular baggage allowance. Battery-powered mobility aids must have the battery disconnected and terminals protected. Lithium-ion batteries must not exceed 300 Wh. Batteries exceeding 160 Wh but not exceeding 300 Wh require airline approval at least 48 hours before departure.
  • Walking aids (canes, crutches, walkers) are transported free of charge and do not count towards the baggage allowance.

3.5. Prohibited Items

The following items are prohibited in both carry-on and checked baggage:

  • Explosives and incendiary substances
  • Compressed gases (except small personal aerosol cans in checked baggage, max 500 ml)
  • Flammable liquids (except personal care items in containers of 100 ml or less in carry-on, or up to 500 ml per container in checked baggage)
  • Radioactive materials
  • Corrosives
  • Poisons and infectious substances

The following items are prohibited in carry-on baggage but permitted in checked baggage:

  • Knives with blades over 6 cm
  • Scissors with blades over 6 cm
  • Tools over 6 cm in length (screwdrivers, wrenches, etc.)
  • Sports equipment that could be used as a weapon (baseball bats, hockey sticks, etc.)

Lithium batteries:

  • Spare lithium batteries must be carried in carry-on baggage only (not checked).
  • Maximum 2 spare batteries per passenger, each not exceeding 160 Wh.
  • Batteries must be individually protected against short circuit.
  • Devices containing lithium batteries (laptops, phones, etc.) are permitted in both carry-on and checked baggage.

3.6. Lost, Delayed, and Damaged Baggage

If checked baggage does not arrive on the carousel at the destination:

  1. The passenger must report the missing baggage immediately at the SkyWay Baggage Service desk in the arrivals hall before leaving the airport. A Property Irregularity Report (PIR) will be created and the passenger will receive a reference number.
  2. SkyWay Airlines will make reasonable efforts to locate and deliver the baggage within 24 hours for domestic flights and within 48 hours for international flights.
  3. If the baggage has not been found within 5 days, the passenger should submit a formal claim through the SkyWay website or Customer Service Centre.
  4. If the baggage is declared lost (not found within 21 days of the PIR), SkyWay Airlines will compensate the passenger up to the limit specified by the Montreal Convention (currently 1,288 SDR, approximately EUR 1,600). Compensation is based on the depreciated value of the contents, not the replacement cost. Passengers are encouraged to keep receipts for valuable items.
  5. For delayed baggage, SkyWay Airlines will reimburse reasonable expenses for essential items (toiletries, basic clothing) up to EUR 100 per day for a maximum of 3 days, upon presentation of receipts.

Damaged baggage:

Damage to checked baggage must be reported at the Baggage Service desk before leaving the airport. If damage is discovered after leaving, it must be reported in writing within 7 days of receipt of the baggage. Claims submitted after 7 days will not be accepted. SkyWay Airlines will repair or replace damaged baggage up to the Montreal Convention limit. Normal wear and tear (scuffs, scratches, dents from handling) is not covered.

Important deadlines:

  • Damaged baggage: report within 7 days of receiving the baggage.
  • Delayed baggage: submit a formal claim within 21 days of the baggage being made available to the passenger.
  • All claims must include the PIR reference number, boarding pass, baggage tag receipt, and photographic evidence of damage (for damage claims).

4. Flight Changes and Cancellations

4.1. Voluntary Changes by the Passenger

Passengers may change the date, time, or route of their booking subject to the rules of their fare class:

Economy Light:

  • Changes permitted up to 24 hours before the original departure time.
  • Change fee: EUR 75 per passenger per segment plus any fare difference.
  • Changes within 24 hours of departure: not permitted. The fare is forfeited.

Economy Standard:

  • Changes permitted up to 24 hours before the original departure time.
  • Change fee: EUR 50 per passenger per segment plus any fare difference.
  • Changes within 24 hours of departure: EUR 100 per segment plus fare difference.

Economy Flex:

  • Free changes up to 3 hours before departure, subject to fare difference.
  • Changes within 3 hours of departure: EUR 50 per segment plus fare difference.

Premium Economy:

  • Free changes up to 24 hours before departure, subject to fare difference.
  • Changes within 24 hours: EUR 50 per segment plus fare difference.

Business Class:

  • Free unlimited changes subject to fare difference only.
  • Changes permitted up to 1 hour before departure.

First Class:

  • Free unlimited changes including date, route, and cabin class (upgrade only).
  • Downgrade: fare difference is refunded.
  • Changes permitted up to 1 hour before departure.

All changes are subject to seat availability on the new flight. If no seats are available in the original fare class, the passenger may be offered a higher fare class at the applicable fare difference or may choose an alternative date or route.

4.2. Voluntary Cancellation by the Passenger

Cancellation and refund rules depend on the fare class:

Economy Light:

  • Non-refundable. No refund is provided for cancellations.
  • Airport taxes and government-imposed fees are refundable upon request.

Economy Standard:

  • Non-refundable as cash. A travel voucher for the fare value (excluding taxes) is issued for cancellations made more than 48 hours before departure. The voucher is valid for 12 months from the date of issue and may be used for any SkyWay booking.
  • Cancellations within 48 hours of departure: no voucher is issued. Airport taxes and government-imposed fees are refundable upon request.

Economy Flex:

  • Fully refundable up to 24 hours before departure. Refund is processed to the original payment method within 7 business days.
  • Cancellations within 24 hours of departure: a travel voucher is issued for the fare value.

Premium Economy:

  • Fully refundable up to 48 hours before departure.
  • Cancellations within 48 hours: a travel voucher is issued for the fare value.

Business Class:

  • Fully refundable at any time before departure.
  • Refund is processed within 7 business days to the original payment method.

First Class:

  • Fully refundable at any time before departure.
  • Refund is processed within 3 business days to the original payment method.

For all fare classes, a no-show (failing to check in or board the flight without prior cancellation) results in forfeiture of the entire fare. No refund, voucher, or rebooking is provided for no-shows, regardless of fare class. If the booking includes a return or onward segment, all subsequent segments are automatically cancelled upon a no-show on any segment.

4.3. Cancellation by SkyWay Airlines

When SkyWay Airlines cancels a flight, passengers are entitled to one of the following, at their choice:

  1. Rebooking on the next available SkyWay flight to the same destination at no extra cost, in the same cabin class. If no SkyWay flight is available within 24 hours, SkyWay will rebook the passenger on a partner airline or competing carrier.
  2. A full refund of the unused portion of the ticket, processed within 7 business days.
  3. Rebooking to a later date of the passenger's choice, subject to availability and at no extra cost.

In addition, if the cancellation is notified less than 14 days before the scheduled departure, passengers may be entitled to compensation under EU Regulation 261/2004 (see Section 5.2).

4.4. Schedule Changes

SkyWay Airlines may change the scheduled departure time of a flight for operational reasons. Passengers will be notified of schedule changes by email and/or SMS using the contact details provided at the time of booking.

  • Changes of less than 2 hours: no additional entitlement.
  • Changes of 2 hours or more: the passenger may accept the new time, rebook free of charge on an alternative SkyWay flight, or cancel for a full refund.

4.5. Connecting Flights and Missed Connections

For passengers booked on connecting flights on a single ticket (a single booking reference covering all segments):

  • If a passenger misses a connection due to a delay or cancellation of the first SkyWay flight, SkyWay Airlines will rebook the passenger on the next available connection at no extra cost and provide care and assistance as required (see Section 5.3).
  • The passenger's checked baggage will be automatically re-tagged and transferred to the new connecting flight.
  • If the delay results in an overnight wait, SkyWay will provide hotel accommodation and transport to and from the hotel.

For passengers who have booked separate tickets for connecting flights (two or more independent booking references):

  • SkyWay Airlines is not responsible for missed connections. Each booking is treated independently. If the first flight is delayed causing the passenger to miss the second flight, no rebooking, compensation, or care is provided for the second booking.
  • Passengers are strongly advised to allow sufficient connection time when booking separate tickets. SkyWay recommends a minimum of 3 hours between separately booked flights.

5. Delays and Disruptions

5.1. Definition of Delay

A flight is considered delayed when it departs later than the scheduled departure time shown on the booking confirmation. Delays are categorized as follows for the purpose of passenger entitlements:

  • Short delay: less than 2 hours.
  • Moderate delay: 2 hours or more but less than 3 hours.
  • Long delay: 3 hours or more but less than 5 hours.
  • Extended delay: 5 hours or more.

5.2. Compensation for Delays and Cancellations (EU Regulation 261/2004)

Passengers departing from an EU/EEA airport, or arriving at an EU/EEA airport on a SkyWay flight, are entitled to compensation under EU Regulation 261/2004 when the flight arrives at the final destination with a delay of 3 hours or more, unless the delay is caused by extraordinary circumstances.

Compensation amounts:

  • EUR 250 for flights up to 1,500 km.
  • EUR 400 for flights between 1,500 km and 3,500 km.
  • EUR 600 for flights over 3,500 km.

The compensation may be reduced by 50% if SkyWay Airlines offers rebooking on an alternative flight that arrives at the final destination within:

  • 2 hours of the original scheduled arrival for flights up to 1,500 km.
  • 3 hours of the original scheduled arrival for flights between 1,500 and 3,500 km.
  • 4 hours of the original scheduled arrival for flights over 3,500 km.

Extraordinary circumstances that exempt SkyWay from compensation obligations include:

  • Severe weather conditions (storms, fog, heavy snow, volcanic ash).
  • Air traffic control restrictions or airport closures.
  • Security threats or political instability.
  • Hidden manufacturing defects not detectable through routine maintenance.
  • Strikes by entities outside SkyWay's control (e.g., air traffic controllers, airport staff). Note: strikes by SkyWay employees are not considered extraordinary circumstances.

5.3. Right to Care and Assistance

Regardless of whether compensation is payable, passengers experiencing delays are entitled to care and assistance as follows:

For delays of 2 hours or more (short-haul flights up to 1,500 km):

  • Complimentary meals and refreshments in reasonable relation to the waiting time.
  • Two free phone calls, emails, or faxes.

For delays of 3 hours or more (medium-haul flights 1,500–3,500 km):

  • Complimentary meals and refreshments.
  • Two free phone calls, emails, or faxes.

For delays of 4 hours or more (long-haul flights over 3,500 km):

  • Complimentary meals and refreshments.
  • Two free phone calls, emails, or faxes.

For delays requiring an overnight stay (when the rescheduled departure is the next day or later):

  • Hotel accommodation including transport between the airport and hotel.

For delays of 5 hours or more:

  • In addition to the above, the passenger may choose to abandon the journey and receive a full refund of the unused portion of the ticket plus, if applicable, a return flight to the original point of departure.

Care and assistance is provided regardless of the cause of the delay, including extraordinary circumstances.

SkyWay distributes care vouchers (meal vouchers, hotel vouchers) at the gate or customer service desk. If vouchers are not distributed and the passenger incurs expenses, SkyWay will reimburse reasonable costs upon submission of receipts within 28 days of the disruption.

5.4. Denied Boarding (Overbooking)

SkyWay Airlines may overbook flights to compensate for expected no-shows. If more passengers hold confirmed reservations than there are seats available, SkyWay will first seek volunteers willing to give up their seats in exchange for compensation (which may include travel vouchers, upgrades on a future flight, or cash).

If there are not enough volunteers, SkyWay may deny boarding to passengers involuntarily. In such cases, the denied passenger is entitled to:

  • Compensation under EU Regulation 261/2004 (see Section 5.2) — the full amount, with no reduction.
  • Rebooking on the next available flight or a full refund.
  • Care and assistance as described in Section 5.3.

Involuntary denied boarding is applied in the following priority order (passengers denied last have the highest priority to keep their seats): 1. Passengers who checked in last. 2. Passengers on the lowest fare class. 3. Passengers without frequent flyer status. Passengers with reduced mobility, unaccompanied minors, and families with young children are never involuntarily denied boarding.

6. SkyWay Voyager Loyalty Program

6.1. Program Overview

SkyWay Voyager is the frequent flyer loyalty program of SkyWay Airlines. Membership is free and open to anyone aged 2 years or older. Members earn Voyager Miles on eligible SkyWay flights and partner services, which can be redeemed for flights, upgrades, and other rewards.

6.2. Earning Voyager Miles

Miles are earned based on the distance flown and the fare class:

  • Economy Light: 0.5 miles per km flown.
  • Economy Standard: 1.0 mile per km flown.
  • Economy Flex: 1.25 miles per km flown.
  • Premium Economy: 1.5 miles per km flown.
  • Business Class: 2.0 miles per km flown.
  • First Class: 3.0 miles per km flown.

Status miles (used for tier qualification) are earned at the same rates. Partner airline flights earn between 0.25 and 2.0 miles per km depending on the partner and fare class.

Non-flight earning opportunities:

  • SkyWay Voyager credit card: 1 mile per EUR 1 spent on everyday purchases; 2 miles per EUR 1 spent on SkyWay purchases.
  • Partner hotels: 500 miles per eligible stay.
  • Partner car rental: 500 miles per eligible rental.

6.3. Tier Levels

SkyWay Voyager has four tier levels, determined by status miles earned in a calendar year:

Blue (base level):

  • Default tier for all new members.
  • Benefits: earn and redeem Voyager Miles, access to member-only fares.
  • No minimum miles required.

Silver (25,000 status miles or 30 eligible flight segments per calendar year):

  • All Blue benefits, plus:
  • Priority check-in and boarding.
  • One additional checked bag (23 kg) on all SkyWay flights, regardless of fare class.
  • Preferred seat selection free of charge in Economy.
  • 25% bonus on base Voyager Miles earned.

Gold (50,000 status miles or 60 eligible flight segments per calendar year):

  • All Silver benefits, plus:
  • SkyWay Lounge access when flying SkyWay (including Economy).
  • Guaranteed economy seat reservation up to 48 hours before departure on sold-out flights.
  • Two additional checked bags (23 kg each) on all SkyWay flights.
  • 50% bonus on base Voyager Miles earned.
  • Complimentary upgrade to the next cabin class, subject to availability, confirmed at check-in.
  • Dedicated Gold hotline with priority wait times (target: under 2 minutes).

Platinum (100,000 status miles or 120 eligible flight segments per calendar year):

  • All Gold benefits, plus:
  • Access to SkyWay First Class lounges when departing from a SkyWay hub airport.
  • Guaranteed Business Class seat reservation up to 24 hours before departure on sold-out flights.
  • Three additional checked bags (32 kg each) on all SkyWay flights.
  • 100% bonus on base Voyager Miles earned.
  • Complimentary upgrade to the next cabin class, confirmed 72 hours before departure (subject to availability).
  • Personal SkyWay Voyager concierge for booking and travel assistance.
  • Chauffeur airport transfer at hub airports when flying Business or First Class.

Tier status is evaluated on 1 January of each year based on miles or segments earned in the previous calendar year. Status is valid for the full following calendar year plus a 3-month grace period (through 31 March). If the required miles or segments are not earned in the evaluation year, the member is downgraded by one tier (not to base).

6.4. Redeeming Voyager Miles

Miles may be redeemed for:

  • Award flights on SkyWay Airlines starting at 10,000 miles for a short-haul Economy return flight. Award seat availability is limited and varies by route and season.
  • Cabin class upgrades: upgrade from Economy to Premium Economy from 15,000 miles per segment; Economy to Business from 30,000 miles; Business to First from 25,000 miles. Subject to availability.
  • Seat selection fees, excess baggage fees, and other ancillary services.
  • Partner redemptions: hotel stays, car rentals, and retail gift cards.

Miles expire 36 months after the month in which they were earned, unless the member has qualifying activity (a SkyWay flight or partner transaction) within that period. Any qualifying activity resets the expiry clock for all accumulated miles.

6.5. Status Matching and Challenges

SkyWay Voyager offers a one-time status match for members holding equivalent status with a competing airline's loyalty program. The matched status is granted for a 90-day trial period, during which the member must earn a specified number of miles to retain the status for the full year:

  • Silver match: earn 6,000 status miles in 90 days.
  • Gold match: earn 12,000 status miles in 90 days.
  • Platinum match: earn 25,000 status miles in 90 days.

If the challenge is not met, the member reverts to Blue tier at the end of the 90-day period. Status match requests must include proof of current status with the competing airline and can be submitted through the SkyWay website.

7. Special Assistance

7.1. Passengers with Reduced Mobility

SkyWay Airlines provides assistance to passengers with reduced mobility at no additional charge. Services include:

  • Wheelchair assistance from the airport entrance to the aircraft seat and from the aircraft seat to the arrivals area at the destination.
  • Assistance with boarding and deplaning, including the use of an aisle chair if needed.
  • Transport of wheelchairs and mobility aids free of charge (see Section 3.4).
  • Seating in accessible rows with additional legroom where available.

Passengers requiring assistance must notify SkyWay at least 48 hours before departure through the Customer Service Centre or at the time of booking. Requests made less than 48 hours before departure will be accommodated on a best-effort basis.

SkyWay staff are trained to assist passengers with reduced mobility, but are not permitted to provide medical assistance, administer medication, or assist with personal care (eating, using the lavatory). Passengers who require such assistance must travel with a personal care attendant.

7.2. Unaccompanied Minors

Children aged 5 to 11 may travel without an accompanying adult on SkyWay flights under the Unaccompanied Minor (UM) service. The service is mandatory for children in this age group and carries a fee of EUR 50 per direction for short-haul flights and EUR 100 per direction for medium-haul and long-haul flights.

Requirements:

  • The UM service must be booked at least 48 hours before departure through the Customer Service Centre (it cannot be booked online).
  • A designated adult must bring the child to the airport check-in counter and remain at the airport until the flight has departed.
  • A designated adult must meet the child at the destination airport at the gate.
  • Both the dropping-off and picking-up adults must present valid photo identification.
  • The child will be accompanied by SkyWay ground staff and cabin crew throughout the journey.
  • Unaccompanied minors are not accepted on flights with connections requiring a change of terminal.

Children aged 12 to 17 may travel alone without the UM service but may optionally use it upon parental request at the same fee.

7.3. Pregnant Passengers

SkyWay Airlines accepts pregnant passengers under the following conditions:

  • Up to 28 weeks of pregnancy: no restrictions, no medical certificate required.
  • From 28 to 36 weeks (single pregnancy) or 28 to 32 weeks (multiple pregnancy): a medical certificate confirming fitness to fly is required. The certificate must be dated no more than 7 days before the date of travel.
  • After 36 weeks (single pregnancy) or after 32 weeks (multiple pregnancy): travel is not permitted.

Pregnant passengers are advised to choose an aisle seat for comfort and easy access to the lavatory. Seat belt extensions are available on request.

7.4. Medical Equipment and Medication

Passengers may carry essential medical equipment and medication in the cabin:

  • Medication: carried in carry-on baggage with no additional fee. Liquid medication exceeding 100 ml is permitted in carry-on if accompanied by a medical certificate or prescription. It must be declared at the security checkpoint.
  • CPAP machines: permitted in the cabin as a medical device, in addition to the standard carry-on allowance. Must be battery-powered for in-flight use.
  • Portable oxygen concentrators (POCs): permitted on SkyWay flights if the device is FAA-approved. The passenger must carry sufficient battery power for the flight duration plus 3 hours. A medical certificate is required, submitted at least 72 hours before departure.
  • Injectable medication and needles: permitted in carry-on with a medical certificate. Used needles must be stored in a sharps container.

7.5. Service Animals

Recognized service animals (guide dogs, hearing dogs, and psychiatric service dogs) are permitted in the cabin at no additional charge on all SkyWay flights. The service animal must:

  • Be trained by an accredited service animal organization.
  • Wear a harness or identifying vest.
  • Remain at the passenger's feet or on the passenger's lap (if small enough) for the duration of the flight.
  • Not occupy a seat.

Documentation required: a certificate of training from an accredited organization and a veterinary health certificate dated within 10 days of travel. Documentation must be presented at check-in.

Emotional support animals are not accepted in the cabin and must travel as cargo if they meet the requirements for live animal transport through SkyWay Cargo.

8. In-flight Services

8.1. Meal Service

Meal service varies by route and cabin class:

Short-haul flights (under 3 hours):

  • Economy: complimentary non-alcoholic beverages; snacks available for purchase (buy-on-board menu starting at EUR 3).
  • Premium Economy: complimentary non-alcoholic beverages and a light snack.
  • Business Class: complimentary beverages (including alcohol) and a light meal.

Medium-haul flights (3–6 hours):

  • Economy: complimentary non-alcoholic beverages and a sandwich or snack box; hot meals available for purchase (EUR 8–12).
  • Premium Economy: complimentary beverages and a hot meal with a choice of two options.
  • Business Class: complimentary beverages and a two-course hot meal.

Long-haul flights (over 6 hours):

  • Economy: complimentary full meal service (one hot meal and one lighter meal or snack for flights over 8 hours) and non-alcoholic beverages; alcoholic beverages for purchase (EUR 5–8).
  • Premium Economy: complimentary full meal service with choice of two entrées, beverages including alcohol.
  • Business Class: complimentary multi-course meal with choice of three entrées, full bar service.
  • First Class: complimentary à la carte dining with choice of five entrées, premium champagne and wine, snack bar available throughout the flight.

Special meals (vegetarian, vegan, halal, kosher, gluten-free, diabetic, low-sodium, child meal, and infant meal) must be requested at least 24 hours before departure through the website, app, or Customer Service Centre. Special meals cannot be guaranteed if requested less than 24 hours before departure.

8.2. In-flight Entertainment

All SkyWay aircraft are equipped with a personal in-flight entertainment (IFE) system on medium-haul and long-haul flights, featuring:

  • Over 200 movies, including new releases and classics.
  • Over 150 TV show episodes.
  • Music channels, podcasts, and audiobooks.
  • Games.
  • Flight map and external camera views.

On short-haul flights, entertainment is available via the SkyWay streaming app (passengers must download the app before departure and use their own device).

Business Class and First Class passengers receive complimentary noise-cancelling headphones. Economy and Premium Economy passengers may bring their own headphones or purchase a SkyWay headset for EUR 3.

8.3. Wi-Fi

Wi-Fi is available on all SkyWay aircraft equipped with satellite connectivity (approximately 80% of the fleet as of March 2026). Wi-Fi packages are:

  • Chat package (messaging apps only): EUR 5 for the full flight.
  • Browse package (web browsing and email): EUR 12 for the full flight.
  • Stream package (full streaming capability): EUR 20 for the full flight.

SkyWay Voyager Gold and Platinum members receive complimentary Browse Wi-Fi on all flights. Business Class passengers receive complimentary Browse Wi-Fi; First Class passengers receive complimentary Stream Wi-Fi.

Wi-Fi availability may be limited or unavailable during certain phases of flight or over certain geographic regions.

9. Contact, Claims, and Feedback

9.1. Customer Service Centre

The SkyWay Airlines Customer Service Centre is available 24/7:

  • Phone: +49 69 555 0100 (international), local-rate numbers available in 30 countries (see www.skyway-airlines.example/contact).
  • Email: customer.service@skyway-airlines.example (response within 5 business days).
  • Live chat: available through the SkyWay website and app from 06:00 to 22:00 CET.
  • Social media: @SkyWayAirlines on Twitter/X and Facebook (response within 4 hours during operating hours).

SkyWay Voyager Gold members have access to a dedicated priority hotline with a target response time of under 2 minutes. Platinum members have access to a personal concierge reachable by phone and email.

9.2. Complaints and Claims Process

Passengers wishing to file a complaint or claim should follow this process:

  1. Submit the complaint through the SkyWay website (preferred) or by email to claims@skyway-airlines.example.
  2. Include the booking reference (PNR), flight number, date of travel, and a description of the issue.
  3. For baggage claims, include the PIR reference number, photos of damage, and receipts for any items claimed.
  4. For delay or cancellation compensation claims under EU261, include boarding pass and evidence of the delay (e.g., screenshot of flight status).

Response times:

  • Acknowledgment of receipt: within 48 hours.
  • Substantive response: within 28 days. If a response cannot be provided within 28 days due to the complexity of the case, the passenger will be informed of the expected timeline.

If the passenger is not satisfied with SkyWay's response, they may escalate to:

  • The national enforcement body in the country of departure (for EU261 claims).
  • The SkyWay Customer Ombudsman (ombudsman@skyway-airlines.example), who provides an independent review.
  • An Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) body. SkyWay participates in the söp (Schlichtungsstelle für den öffentlichen Personenverkehr) in Germany and equivalent bodies in other EU member states.

9.3. Refund Processing

Refund processing times vary by payment method:

  • Credit card: 7 business days (may take additional time to appear on the statement).
  • Bank transfer: 14 business days.
  • Travel voucher: issued immediately by email.

Refunds for tickets purchased through travel agents or OTAs are processed through the original booking channel. The passenger should contact their agent or OTA for refund status.
